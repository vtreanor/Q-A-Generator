Bus & Truck Theory Test

Hard to remember questions

August 2021

Vincent Treanor

Notes

Text in brackets are explanations, not part of the answer

There are inconsistancies but, these quesstions and answers are the official ones.

1. How long may a driver continue to drive without a driver card if their card has been lost or stolen?

15 days

2. What should a driver do with a driver card that has malfunctioned?

Return it to the Road Safety Authority

3. How often must the data on a driver card be downloaded?

28 days

4. Who should a driver notify if his tachograph card is lost or stolen?

Gardai and RSA.

5. Who is responsible for the long term storage of tachograph data after 21 days?

The Operator.

6. Who should you notify if the tachograph is damaged?

RSA

7. According to EU drivers regulations a break is required for a driver after driving...

4½ hours

8. Under EU drivers regulations, what is the minimum number of consecutive hours of daily rest a driver must take?

11 reduced to 9
(EU regulations specify that a driver must take an 11 hour rest period within each 24 hour period. This may be reduced to 9 hours on a maximum of 3 days in a two week period.)

9. After 4½ hours of driving, how long a break must a driver take?

45 minutes

10. What is the maximum time a driver can drive between weekly rest periods?

56 hours

11. Under EU driving hours regulations, what is the maximum hours that may be driven in a given week?

56 hours

12. Under EU driving hours regulations, the shortest break that a driver can take in a driviing period is?

15 minutes

13. Under EU drivers hours regulations, a driver must take a break after how many hours driving?

4½ hours

14. Under EU drivers hours regulations, after driving for 4½ hours, a driver must take an uninterrupted break of ...

45 minutes.

15. What is the maximum number of hours a driver can drive without taking a break? ::

4½ hours.

16. What is the maximum number of hours a driver can drive in a given week?

56 hours.

17. What is the maximum number of driving hours in a two week period? ::

90 hours.

18. What is the minimum break time that should be taken for each break during or following a driving period?

15 minutes.

19. What is the minimum break time which must be taken following a 4½ hour driving period?

45 minutes.

20. What is the maximum number of hours a driver may drive in week two, if 56 hours were worked in week one? ::

34 hours.

21. On how many days of the working week may a driver drive for more than 9 hours?

2 days.

22. On how many days of the working week may a driver drive?

6 days.

23. On 3 days of the week what can the minimum daily rest period be reduced to?

9 hours.
(EU regulations specify that a driver must take an 11 hour rest period within each 24 hour period. This may be reduced to 9 hours on a maximum of 3 days in a two week period.)

24. What is the normal minimum weekly rest period?

45 consecutive hours.

25. On a rest day, may a driver carry out other 'paid duties' apart from driving?

No, it is a rest day.

26. Who enforces the licensing provisions of the Road Transport Acts?

The RSA and Gardai.

27. Road Haulage and Road Passenger Transport Operator's license are issued and maintained by ...

The Department of Transport.

28. Which of the following organisations is responsible for enforcing EU and national transport legislation on the licensing of road haulage and passenger operators?

The RSA

29. What operator is exempt from carrying a Road Haulage Operators License?

An operator who owns a category C type truck and uses it to transport their own goods.

30. How many penalty points collected in 36 months will result in a driving ban?

12 points

31. If a fixed charge notice is received, the fine must be paid within how many days to avoid additional penalty?

28 days.

32. A fixed charge contains all the following information EXCEPT...

when to appear in court.

33. If 12 or more panalty points are built up in a 36 month period, how many months is the driver banned from driving?

6 months.
(and, you must hand in your driving license at the motor tax office within 14 days.)

34. If a driver is disqualified from driving, within how many days must the license be handed in to the court for endorsement?

14 days.

35. What distance before a zebra crossing is parking prohibited?

15 metres.

36. Within what distance of a road junction is parking prohibited?

5 metres, unless parking spaces are clearly marked.

37. A driver has driven a vehicle on a journey and has not used a tachograph. What is the MOST severe penalty risk?

Loss of license and heavy fines.

38. A driver did not use a tachograph chart on a new journey. Which of the following is the MOST severe penalty risk?

Imprisonment and/or heavy fine.

39. How many road deaths in Ireland in 2017?

158

40. A driver using a mobile phone is how many times more likely to have an incident?

4 times

41. Is a driver allowed to sound the horn while driving in a built up area at night?

Yes, but between 11:30pm and 07:00am the horn may be sounded only in an emergency

42. When is the use of the horn prohibited?

Between 11:30pm and 07:00am in a built up area

43. What stopping distance should a driver allow for when driving in snow or icy conditions?

Up to 10 times the normal distance

44. In wet weather how might a driver judge what is a safe following distance from the vehicle in front?

By allowing at least 4 seconds to elapse between the vehicle in front and the driver's own vehicle passing a fixed point

45. What are the drink driving penalties for a learner driver with a blood alcohol concentrate (BAC) of 20 mg to 80 mg per 100 millilitres of blood?

€200 fine and 3 months driving disqualification

46. What are the drink driving penalties for a professional driver holding a full car license and found to have a blood alcohol concentrate (BAC) of 20 mg to 80 mg per 100 millilitres of blood?

€200 fine and 3 months driving disqualification

47. What are the drink driving penalties for a driver with a blood alcohol concentrate (BAC) of 80 mg to 100 mg per 100 milliolitres of blood?

€400 fine and 6 months driving disqualification

48. what is the penalty for a driver who refuses to give breath, blood or urine samples as evidence for a second offence to An Garda Siochana?

Automatic disqualification for 6 years.

49. What is the maximum penalty on conviction of drug driving?

€5000 fine and 6 months in prison.

50. What is the recommended minimum stopping distance for a car travelling at 50 km/h on a dry road?

25 metres

51. What is the recommended minimum stopping distance for a car travelling at 50 km/h on a wet road?

35 metres

52. What is the recommended minimum stopping distance for a car travelling at 100 km/h on a wet road?

70 metres

53. What is the rule relating to the 'safe headway' (a safe distance between two vehicles on the road) when weather conditions are good?

The 2 second rule

54. In which of the following situations is overtaking on the left permitted?

The overtaking driver has signalled that they intend to turn left.

55. A learner permit driver who receives 7 penalty points in a 36 month period (3 years) will be banned from driving for how many months?

6 months

56. What is the minimum penalty point offence for a learner driver not displaying 'L' plates both on the front and rear of the vehicle?

2 penalty points

57. How many penalty points will an unaccompanied driver receive on conviction?

4 penalty points

58. What is the fine for a learner driver for driving a vehicle unaccompanied?

€120

59. A learner driver who is stopped by a Garda for driving unaccompanied facesa a minimum fine of how much?

€80

60. If the owner of a vehicle allows a learner driver to drive their vehicle, what is the maximum fine the owner could face?

€1000

61. What is the fixed charge amount and penalties incurred for a learner driver who drived unaccompanied?

€80 fine and 2 penalty points

62. A learner driver detected of driving unaccompanied faces a minimum of how many penalty points?

2 penalty points

63. On average, how many pedestrians will be killed if hit by a car at 60 km/h?

9 out of 10

64. On average, how many pedestrians will be killed if hit by a car at 50 km/h?

5 out of 10

65. On average, how many pedestrians will be killed if hit by a car at 30 km/h?

1 out of 10

66. What category of license must a driver hold in order to drive a vehicle Maximum Authorised Mass (MAM) of 7500 kg with seatin for no more than 8 passengers?

C1, C1E, C or CE

67. What is the Maximum Authorised Mass (MAM) of a vehicle that the holder of a category C1 license may drive?

7500 kg

68. Is the holder of a category C1 license entitled to tow a trailer?

Yes, uo to 750 kg Maximum Authorised Mass (MAM)

69. Unless otherwise indicated, what is the maximum permissible speed of a car towing a trailer on national roads?

80 km/h

70. Unless otherwise indicated, what is the maximum permissible speed of a car towing a trailer on a motorway?

80 km/h

71. What is the maximum permitted speed of a truck?

90 km/h

72. What is the maximum permitted speed of a truck on a motorway?

90 km/h

73. What is the maximum permitted speed of a truck on a national road?

80 km/h

74. What is the maximum permitted speed of a truck on a dual carriageway?

80 km/h

75. What is the maximum permitted speed on a motorway for a goods vehicle with a Maximum Authorised Mass (MAM) of more than 3500 kg?

90 km/h

76. What is the maximum permitted speed on a motorway for a goods vehicle with a Maximum Authorised Mass (MAM) of 3000 kg?

120 km/h

77. What is the maximum permitted rear load overhang with a red flag ?

3 metres

78. What is the maximum permitted rear load overhang which does not require a red flag ?

1 metre

79. What is the maximum permitted length of an articulated vehicle ?

16.5 metres

80. What is the Maximum Authorised Mass (MAM) in tonnes of a 4 axle rigid truck with conventional (non air) suspension ?

30 tonnes

81. What is the Maximum Authorised Mass (MAM) in tonnes of a 3 axle rigid truck with conventional (non air) suspension ?

25 tonnes

82. What is the Maximum Authorised Mass (MAM) in tonnes of a 2 axle rigid truck with conventional suspension ?

18 tonnes

83. A category D1E license is required to drive which of the following vehicles ?

A vehicle that carries more than 8 passengers but not more than 16 excluding the driver, towing a trailer, where the combined Maximum Authorised Mass (MAM) is 12,000 kg or less
